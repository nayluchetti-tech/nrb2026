Lead Pipeline: 4-Stage Process

1

Capture

Badge scan, QR code, business card photo, badge photo, audio note, or form submission. Get into Google Sheets within minutes.

2

Enrich

Automatically pull: company type, size, media presence, NRB role (exhibitor/speaker), and past PRAY.COM interactions.

3

Classify

Tier the lead using demographics + buy intent: MOMENTUM (meeting at NRB), HOT (meeting after NRB), WARM (need to qualify), or DISQUALIFIED.

4

Communicate

Trigger the right sequence: same-day text, next-morning email, post-NRB follow-up, or nurture campaign.

Someone passes by the booth and we manage to scan their NRB attendee badge. No conversation.

1 Capture Method

QR Self-Service Scan Medium Intent

The attendee proactively scans the PRAY QR code on banners to book a meeting immediately or for a later date.

1 Capture Method
  • 1 Attendee scans the PRAY.COM QR code on booth banners, table displays, or handout materials

Meaningful Conversation + Demo Interest Momentum

Deep conversation with booth staff, expressed interest in a demo. Highest-value interaction.

1 Capture Methods (via Lead Capture Tool)
  • A Primary: Audio Note — AE opens the Lead Capture Tool, selects "Audio Note" tab, and records a voice note with the person's name, company, website, and conversation details
  • > AI transcribes and structures the data, then sends it to the NRB 2026 Lead Tracker Google Sheet automatically
  • B Plus: Badge/Card Photos — In the same tool, AE also attaches a photo of the badge QR or business card (built-in camera capture). Photos are saved to Google Drive with links in the spreadsheet
  • C Meeting Notes: AE fills in pain points, next steps, products discussed, demo status, and meeting quality rating (1-5) in the structured form
  • D Alternative: Type It In — AE can also use the "Type It In" tab to manually enter all lead details directly if audio isn't convenient
Critical: This is our highest-value scenario. Double-capture (audio/typed + badge/card photo) is mandatory. AEs must record notes within 15 minutes of the conversation ending.

Pre-Booked Meeting High Intent

Attendee pre-booked a meeting before or during NRB. Track show/no-show and follow up accordingly.

1 Tracking Process
  • 1 Pre-event: Contact already in the Google Sheet and HubSpot from form submission or Calendly booking with meeting time confirmed
  • 2 Day of meeting: Booth manager checks the daily meeting roster and confirms attendee arrivals
  • 3 Showed: Mark meeting_status = completed in the Google Sheet. AE records meeting notes via the Lead Capture Tool (Audio, Photo, or Type It In).
  • 4 No-Show: Mark meeting_status = no_show. Trigger no-show follow-up sequence.
  • 5 AE adds meeting notes via the Lead Capture Tool: how it went, what was discussed, follow-up actions, deal potential rating (1-5)
Booth Manager Role: Print the daily meeting roster each morning. Check off arrivals in real-time. At end of day, bulk-update Google Sheet and HubSpot statuses.
2 Data Tracked
  • Meeting Status
    completed / no_show / rescheduled
  • Meeting Notes
    Conversation summary, products demoed, objections, next steps
  • Deal Potential
    1 (Long shot) to 5 (Ready to close)
  • Follow-Up Type
    Proposal, Virtual Demo, Contract, Nurture
  • Decision Timeline
    Immediate / 30 days / 90 days / Unknown
  • Source Tag
    nrb_2026_prebooked

D

Lead Classification Decision Tree

Every lead is tiered using two axes: demographics (are they qualified?) and buy intent (are they ready?). This determines whether we pursue a meeting at NRB, after NRB, or not at all.

Two-Axis Scoring Model

Demographics tell us if they're a fit. Buy intent tells us when to act.

Axis 1: Demographics (Qualified?)

  • QUALIFIED = Distributes via Broadcast TV, Radio, or Satellite — OR $60K+/yr marketing spend
  • POSSIBLE = Digital distribution (YouTube, Podcast, Streaming), revenue $1M+, shows pain points — needs more data
  • NOT QUALIFIED = No distribution, revenue under $1M, no aligned pain points, wrong org type

Axis 2: Buy Intent (Ready?)

  • URGENCY = Asked about pricing, requested demo, wants proposal, NRB exhibitor/speaker (can meet in person), pre-booked meeting
  • INTEREST = Engaged in conversation, asked questions, expressed need, but no commitment to next steps
  • NONE = Badge scan only, "just looking," no conversation, no expressed need

MOMENTUM

Meet at NRB

Qualified + urgency signals. Strike while they're here.

  • QUALIFIED (paid TV/Radio/Satellite or $60K+ spend)
  • AND has urgency: asked about pricing, requested demo, wants a proposal
  • OR is NRB speaker/exhibitor (can meet them in person this week)
  • Decision maker: C-Suite, Pastor/Ministry Leader, Executive Director
  • Revenue $5M+ with strong media presence
  • Meeting quality 3+ (if Scenario 4/5)

Sequence:

  • Instant: Slack/SMS briefing to AE with enriched data + action items
  • Within 30 min: Personal email from AE + SMS
  • Same day: LinkedIn connect + visit their booth if exhibitor
  • Day +1 (during NRB): Push for 2nd in-person meeting
  • Day +2: Executive escalation if no response
  • Day +3 (post-NRB): Tailored proposal or custom deck

HOT

Meet after NRB (urgent)

Qualified but no in-person urgency. Book them within 2 weeks post-event.

  • QUALIFIED (paid TV/Radio/Satellite or $60K+ spend)
  • Showed interest but no immediate commitment at NRB
  • Not an NRB exhibitor/speaker (or couldn't schedule at event)
  • Right org type and revenue ($5M+) but needs follow-up to close
  • "Check with my team" or "send me more info" energy
  • Has pain points that map directly to PRAY products

Sequence:

  • Same day: Personal email from AE referencing conversation
  • Day +1: SMS follow-up + tailored resource (case study, ROI calc)
  • Day +3 (post-NRB): Direct meeting request with calendar link
  • Day +5: Executive intro if no response
  • Day +10: Final push — custom proposal or growth audit offer

WARM

Qualify & nurture

Unsure if qualified, or qualified but not ready to buy. Need to learn more.

  • Qualification unclear: digital-only distribution, revenue unknown or $1M–$5M
  • OR QUALIFIED but no buy signals (not ready, no pain points expressed)
  • Had a conversation but no specific product interest
  • Needs to "think about it" — no timeline
  • Not a decision maker (passed along by someone else)
  • QR self-service scan with limited form data

Sequence:

  • Same day evening: Friendly email — "great to connect at NRB"
  • Day +2: Social proof email (case study, partner metrics)
  • Day +5: Value-add resource + soft CTA to book a call
  • Day +14: If engaged → upgrade to HOT. If not → monthly newsletter drip

DISQUALIFIED

No active pursuit

Not a fit. Don't spend AE time here.

  • Not qualified: no distribution channels, no paid media, wrong org type
  • Revenue under $1M with no growth signals
  • Badge scan only, no conversation, no engagement
  • No pain points that map to PRAY products
  • Competitor employee, student, job seeker, or vendor
  • Explicitly said "not interested" or "wrong fit"

Sequence:

  • Day +1: Generic "thanks for stopping by NRB" email
  • Day +7: Add to general PRAY.COM newsletter (no sales touches)
  • Ongoing: If they engage with 3+ newsletter emails, re-evaluate for WARM
