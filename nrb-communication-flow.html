Lead Pipeline: 4-Stage Process

1

Capture

Badge scan, QR code, business card photo, badge photo, audio note, or form submission. Get into Google Sheets within minutes.

2

Enrich

Automatically pull: company type, size, media presence, NRB role (exhibitor/speaker), and past PRAY.COM interactions.

3

Classify

Score the lead: HOT (pursue meeting at NRB), WARM (post-NRB meeting), or NURTURE (long-term drip sequence).

4

Communicate

Trigger the right sequence: same-day text, next-morning email, post-NRB follow-up, or nurture campaign.

1

Badge Scan Walk-By Low Intent

Someone passes by the booth and we manage to scan their NRB attendee badge. No conversation.

1 Capture Method
  • 1 Booth staff scans the attendee's NRB badge QR code using the official NRB Lead Retrieval app
  • 2 Scan data lands in the NRB system (name, email, title, company, domain)
  • 3 Internal team downloads the CSV batch from NRB system every 30 minutes during booth hours
  • 4 CSV is bulk-uploaded to the NRB 2026 Lead Tracker Google Sheet and to HubSpot, mapped to contact properties
Cadence: Assign one marketing ops person to export/import every 30 min during Day 1-2, then hourly on Day 3-4.

QR Self-Service Scan Medium Intent

The attendee proactively scans the PRAY QR code on banners to book a meeting immediately or for a later date.

1 Capture Method
  • 1 Attendee scans the PRAY.COM QR code on booth banners, table displays, or handout materials
  • 2 QR redirects to the NRB Landing Page (index.html) with the booking form
  • 3 Attendee fills in: name, email, phone, organization, company website, role, budget, interest, preferred time
  • 4 Form data flows to the NRB 2026 Lead Tracker Google Sheet and HubSpot — contact is created/updated instantly with all properties

QR Placement: Main booth entrance banner, demo station table tents, handout one-pagers, AE business cards, and the booth backdrop wall. Every surface = a capture point.

Meaningful Conversation + Demo Interest High Intent

Deep conversation with booth staff, expressed interest in a demo. Highest-value interaction.

1 Capture Methods (via Lead Capture Tool)
  • A Primary: Audio Note — AE opens the Lead Capture Tool, selects "Audio Note" tab, and records a voice note with the person's name, company, website, and conversation details
  • > AI transcribes and structures the data, then sends it to the NRB 2026 Lead Tracker Google Sheet automatically
  • B Plus: Badge/Card Photos — In the same tool, AE also attaches a photo of the badge QR or business card (built-in camera capture). Photos are saved to Google Drive with links in the spreadsheet
  • C Meeting Notes: AE fills in pain points, next steps, products discussed, demo status, and meeting quality rating (1-5) in the structured form
  • D Alternative: Type It In — AE can also use the "Type It In" tab to manually enter all lead details directly if audio isn't convenient
Critical: This is our highest-value scenario. Double-capture (audio/typed + badge/card photo) is mandatory. AEs must record notes within 15 minutes of the conversation ending.

Pre-Booked Meeting High Intent

Attendee pre-booked a meeting before or during NRB. Track show/no-show and follow up accordingly.

1 Tracking Process
  • 1 Pre-event: Contact already in the Google Sheet and HubSpot from form submission or Calendly booking with meeting time confirmed
  • 2 Day of meeting: Booth manager checks the daily meeting roster and confirms attendee arrivals
  • 3 Showed: Mark meeting_status = completed in the Google Sheet. AE records meeting notes via the Lead Capture Tool (Audio, Photo, or Type It In).
  • 4 No-Show: Mark meeting_status = no_show. Trigger no-show follow-up sequence.
  • 5 AE adds meeting notes via the Lead Capture Tool: how it went, what was discussed, follow-up actions, deal potential rating (1-5)
Booth Manager Role: Print the daily meeting roster each morning. Check off arrivals in real-time. At end of day, bulk-update Google Sheet and HubSpot statuses.
2 Data Tracked
  • Meeting Status
    completed / no_show / rescheduled
  • Meeting Notes
    Conversation summary, products demoed, objections, next steps
  • Deal Potential
    1 (Long shot) to 5 (Ready to close)
  • Follow-Up Type
    Proposal, Virtual Demo, Contract, Nurture
  • Decision Timeline
    Immediate / 30 days / 90 days / Unknown
  • Source Tag
    nrb_2026_prebooked

D

Lead Classification Decision Tree

Based on capture scenario + enrichment data, every lead is classified into one of three tracks.

HOT: Pursue Meeting at NRB

Goal: Close or advance a deal before the conference ends

  • Scenario 4 or 5 with positive meeting quality (3+)
  • Intent Level = Hot (asked about pricing or specific product)
  • QUALIFIED: Distributes via Broadcast TV, Radio, or Satellite — or $60K+ marketing spend
  • Decision maker (C-Suite Executive, Founder, Pastor/Ministry Leader)
  • Estimated Revenue $5M+ or strong media presence
  • Asked for a proposal or follow-up meeting

Action:

Same-day personal follow-up from AE. Push for second meeting at NRB. AE visits their booth if exhibitor. Executive escalation if needed.

WARM: Post-NRB Meeting

Goal: Schedule a virtual meeting within 2 weeks of NRB

  • Scenario 2, 3, or 4 with moderate engagement
  • Intent Level = Warm (interested, no commitment yet)
  • Revenue $1M-$5M, or digital-only distribution (YouTube, podcast, streaming)
  • Interested but didn't commit to next steps
  • Needs to "check with their team" or "discuss internally"
  • NRB exhibitor or speaker (can re-engage during event)
  • Has pain points aligned with PRAY products (consolidation, ROI, engagement)

Action:

Same-day email with resource. Post-NRB meeting request within 3-5 days. Send tailored content based on interest area.

NURTURE: Long-Term Drip

Goal: Stay top of mind until they're ready

  • Scenario 1 (badge scan only, no conversation)
  • Intent Level = Cold (just browsing / no product interest)
  • Revenue under $1M, free platforms only, or just getting started
  • Not QUALIFIED (no paid distribution channels)
  • Browsing / "just looking" energy, no specific pain points
  • Not the right fit today but could grow into ICP

Action:

3-email NRB follow-up sequence over 2 weeks. Then enroll in general PRAY.COM monthly nurture (newsletter, product updates, webinar invites, case studies).

Reclassification Rule: If a NURTURE lead engages with 2+ emails (opens + clicks) within the first week, auto-upgrade to WARM and notify the AE team. If a WARM lead requests a demo or responds positively to outreach, upgrade to HOT. Classification is dynamic — review and adjust daily during NRB, weekly after.
